Top Collectibles

Among the most sought-after blind-box lines are Smiskis, the glow-in-the-dark little creatures available in popular sets like the Sunday, Museum, and Hippers series—each box hiding one of several charming pocket pals. Sonny Angel figures have skyrocketed in popularity; these tiny cherub-like dolls in themes like Animal, Birthday Gift Bear, and Hippers series fetch must-have status among collectors thanks to their novelty headgear and secret editions. Skullpanda blends whimsical design with everyday utility—featured in Pop Mart’s Sound, Warmth, and Addams Family series, these cable-dangling pandas double as décor and functional accessories. Peach Riot brings bold color and attitude to the blind-box scene with its Rise Up series of 12 collectible vinyl figures (including chase variants), each figure standing around 4–5 inches tall and perfect for desks or shelf displays. Finally, Hirono’s artist-designed figurines explore emotional nuance and narrative—Pop Mart’s Shelter and Tapestry collections showcase 12 unique designs per set, often including rare “secret” pieces that drive the thrill of the unknown.

What to do with your buddies!

So you’ve finally got your collection of little figures—now comes the fun part! You can just line them up on a shelf or tuck them safely into a keepsake box, but if you’re feeling crafty, stick them into a cute book nook or shadow box to give ’em a mini world of their own. Some people even whip up tiny accessories—little hats, scarves, or ring‐holders—so their figures can rock different looks or hold your favorite jewelry. And don’t forget about everyday carry: clip a Smiski or two onto your phone case, keychain, or backpack zipper. Speaking of Smiski, their new “Hippers” series is perfect for hooking onto phones, computer monitors, and pretty much anything you want to spice up with a sneaky little buddy.



Popular spots to find mystery boxes

If you’re on the hunt for blind boxes, you can’t go wrong with spots like Miniso! They always seem to have fresh series at super reasonable prices. Kinokuniya is a treasure trove if you want something a bit more niche or imported, with shelves full of Japanese and anime-themed surprises. Of course, Pop Mart’s official stores (and their online shop) are the go-to for all the latest series drops and exclusive collabs. Beyond those, you’ll also find cool blind-box selections at specialty toy boutiques, hobby shops, and even places like Urban Outfitters or Barnes & Noble when they host their collectible pop-ups.
